About

Paris, the capital of France, is a prominent European city renowned as a global hub for art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture. The city's 19th-century architecture is characterized by broad boulevards and the River Seine running through it. In addition to iconic landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and the Gothic Notre-Dame Cathedral, Paris is famous for its vibrant cafe scene and high-end boutiques along Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.

Climate

Located in western Europe near the sea, Paris enjoys a mild climate due to the Gulf Stream. The weather can be unpredictable, especially in winter and spring, with sharp, cold winds. The annual average temperature is around 12 °C (lower 50s F), with July averaging 19 °C (upper 60s F) and January around 3 °C (upper 30s F). The temperature drops below freezing for about a month each year, with occasional snowfall. Paris has reduced air pollution, and its water purification system ensures safe drinking water.

The Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, a symbol of both admiration and intrigue, always leaves a lasting impression. Steeped in a history of continuous innovation, it offers a wealth of fascinating information to explore.
